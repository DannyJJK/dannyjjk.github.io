Fire breaks out at Ysgol John Bright

Witnesses report devastating damage at a school in Llandudno.

walesonline
  • Bookmark
  • Share
  • Comments
By
Holly Morgan
  • Updated17:02, 18 OCT 2025
  • Bookmark

    • Flames broke out late this morning at Ysgol John Bright in Llandudno, sending thick plumes of smoke into the sky and prompting an immediate emergency response from firefighters and local police.

    Crews from Conwy and neighbouring fire stations were on scene within minutes of the first 999 calls, battling the blaze as staff evacuated pupils to a nearby assembly point on Maesdu Road. Emergency services confirmed that the evacuation was orderly and that teachers followed the school's fire procedures.

    Witnesses described seeing orange flames through windows on the school's upper floor and several fire engines with ladders fighting the blaze from different angles. Roads around the school were temporarily closed while crews worked to secure the area and keep bystanders at a safe distance.

    The school has opened a support line for concerned parents and is coordinating with the local authority to arrange temporary accommodation for classes if needed.

    Paramedics were present on site as a precaution but there have been no reports of any life-threatening injuries, according to emergency services on the scene.

    Fire investigators have begun an initial examination of the origin of the fire. At this stage, officers said it would be premature to speculate about the cause and stressed that inquiries are ongoing. Police also asked members of the public to avoid speculation and to allow investigators to carry out their work without interference.

    Local councillors and education officials expressed concern and praised the rapid response from emergency services. "We will be offering all necessary support to Ysgol John Bright and affected families," a spokesperson for Conwy County Borough Council said.

    As containment efforts continued into the afternoon, residents in surrounding streets were advised to keep windows closed and to follow any instructions from emergency services. Officials urged anyone with information about the incident to contact North Wales Police to assist the investigation.

    Further updates will be provided as authorities confirm more information about the cause of the fire and the extent of the damage.
